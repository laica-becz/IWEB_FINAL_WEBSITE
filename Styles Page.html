Guide to different Bonsai styles

Explore the traditional artistic forms that define bonsai aesthetics. Each style represents a distinct approach to shaping these miniature trees, from upright and formal to cascading and naturalistic. Understanding these classic styles will deepen your appreciation for the artistry and intention behind each bonsai creation.

Broom style Bonsai (Hokkidachi)

The Formal Upright style features a straight trunk with balanced branches, emphasizing strength and harmony. Maintain it through regular pruning and wire guiding.

Formal Upright Bonsai style (Chokkan)

The Formal Upright is a common bonsai style found in nature where trees grow in full light without competition. It features a straight trunk with clear tapering.

Informal Upright Bonsai style (Moyogi)

Informal Upright bonsai grows in a gentle S-shape, with branches that curve and a trunk that tapers from thick at the base to thin at the top.

Cascade Bonsai style (Kengai)

Cascade bonsai grows in tall pots, starting upright before bending downward at an angle, with branches alternating along the curves.

Literati Bonsai style (Bunjingi)

This style grows tall and slender, with branches only at the top, as the tree competes for sunlight in dense forests.
