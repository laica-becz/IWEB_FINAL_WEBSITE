Guide to Different Bonsai Styles

Explore the traditional artistic forms that define bonsai aesthetics. Each style represents a distinct approach to shaping and training trees, from structured formal designs to flowing natural expressions. Understanding these classic styles will deepen your appreciation for the artistry and intention behind each bonsai creation.

Broom Style Bonsai (Hokidachi)

The Broom Style features a rounded canopy, resembling a broom. Itâ€™s known for its formal yet graceful shape, achieved through pruning and wiring.

Formal Upright Bonsai Style (Chokkan)

The Formal Upright style features a straight trunk with balanced, evenly spaced branches, representing strength and order.

Informal Upright Bonsai Style (Moyogi)

The Informal Upright style has a gently curving trunk with branches positioned at each curve, creating a natural, balanced look.

Cascade Bonsai Style (Kengai)

Cascade bonsai grows downward, resembling a tree in nature that has been forced to grow at an angle, often in mountainous regions.

Literati Bonsai Style (Bunjingi)

This style features tall, thin trunks with sparse foliage at the top, emulating the shape of trees growing in harsh conditions with limited resources.
